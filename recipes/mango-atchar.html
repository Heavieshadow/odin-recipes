Mango Atchar

A South African Mango Pickle

A spicy, tangy, oil-based pickle made with green mangoes. This snack is a South African classic with Indian roots.

Ingredients

This is what you'll need.

  • 4 green (unripe) mangoes, peeled and cut into chunks
  • 29.6 ml coarse salt
  • 118 1/4 ml sunflower or canola oil
  • 29.6 ml brown sugar
  • 6 garlic cloves, finely minced
  • 14 3/4 ml fresh ginger, grated
  • 14 3/4 ml ground coriander
  • 4.9 ml ground cumin
  • 2 1/2 ml turmeric
  • 4.9 ml chilli powder (or to taste)
  • 4.9 ml mustard seeds
  • 2 1/2 fenugreek seeds
  • 29.6 ml white or apple cider vinegar

How to make Mango Atchar

This is the step by step guide to the final product.

  • Toss the mango chunks with 29.6 ml coarse salt, spread on a tray, and leave uncovered for at least 2 hours to draw out moisture. Pat dry with a clean cloth.
  • Heat a dry pan over medium heat. Add 4.9 ml mustard seeds and 2 1/2 ml fenugreek seeds and toast until the mustard seeds begin to pop, about 1-2 minutes. REmove and set aside.
  • Heat 118 1/4 ml sunflower or canola oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add 6 garlic cloves, finely minced and add 14 3/4 ml fresh ginger, grated and fry for 2 minutes until fragrant.
  • Stir in 14 3/4 ground coriander, 4.9 ml ground cumin, 2 1/2 ml turmeric, and 4.9 ml chilli powder, along with the toasted seeds. Cook for another minute.
  • Add the salted, dried mango chunks to the spiced oil. Stir in 29.6 ml brown sugar and 29.6 ml wgite or apple cider vinegar. MIx well to coat all the mango pieces evenly.
  • Reduce heat to low and simmer gently, stirring occasionally, until the mango softens slightly and the oil rises to the top, about 15-20 minutes. the mango should still have a little bite.
  • Pour into sterilised glass jars while hot. Seal and allow to cool completely. Rest for at least 2-3 days before eating. Store in a cool, dark place.

Tips

  • The greener and firmer the mango, the better.
  • Make sure the mango is submerged in oil in the jar to preserve it.
  • Keeps for up to 3 months sealed, or 4 weeks in the fridge once opened.
