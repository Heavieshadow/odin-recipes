Fat Cakes

Traditional fried dough balls

Crispy on the outside, soft and fluffy inside. This classic is popular as a street food and breakfast treat.

Ingredients

This is what you'll need.

  • 709 3/4 ml all-purpose flour
  • 9 7/8 ml instant dry yeast
  • 29.6 ml sugar
  • 4.9 ml salt
  • 236 5/8 ml water (about 40 degrees celcius)
  • 709 3/4 ml vegetable oil (for frying)

How to make Fat Cakes

Step by step guide to the final product

  • In a large bowl, combine 709 3/4 ml all-purpose flour, 9 7/8 ml instant dry yeast, 29.6 ml sugar, and 4.9 ml salt. Whisk together until evenly mixed.
  • Gradually add 236 5/8 ml warm water to the dry ingredients, stirring with a wooden spoon or your hands until a soft, slightly dough forms. Don't overwork it - mix just until combined.
  • Cover the bowl with a clean cloth or plastic wrap and leave in a warm place to rise until doubled in size.
  • Pour 709 3/4 ml vegetable oil (for frying) into a deep pot or pan to about 3-4 cm depth. Heat over medium heat until it reaches 175 degrees celcius. To test without a thermometer, drop in a small piece of dough - it should sizzle and float immediately.
  • With lightly oiled hands, pinch off gold ball-sized pieces of dough and gently flatten them slightly into thick rounds.
  • carefully lower 3-4 fat cakes into the hot oil at a time. Fry until deep golden brown onn the bottom, then flip and fry the other side.
  • Remove fat cakes with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve warm as-is, with jam, honey, butter, or alongside a savory dish.
